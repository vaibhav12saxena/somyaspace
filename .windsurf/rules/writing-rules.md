# Writing and Tone Rules

## Tone

The writing should sound:

* confident
* thoughtful
* human
* concise
* observant
* design focused

Avoid sounding corporate, robotic, or AI generated.

---

# Strict Writing Rules

Never use:

* hyphens connecting thoughts
* em dashes
* buzzwords
* exaggerated claims
* generic UX language
* motivational tone

Avoid phrases like:

* seamless experience
* intuitive interface
* user centric
* leverage
* robust solution
* cutting edge
* enhanced workflow
* streamlined process

---

# Sentence Style

Use:

* short to medium sentences
* natural rhythm
* observational writing
* specific details
* clear reasoning

Prefer:
"The design tool felt visually heavy during editing."

Instead of:
"The interface was redesigned to create a more streamlined and intuitive experience."

---

# Portfolio Case Study Style

Write like a designer reflecting on decisions.

Focus on:

* what was difficult
* tradeoffs
* constraints
* system thinking
* reasoning
* collaboration
* outcomes

Avoid sounding like marketing copy.

---

# Formatting Rules

Prefer:

* smaller paragraphs
* section breaks
* bullets for clarity
* concise headings

Avoid:

* giant text blocks
* filler sentences
* repetitive wording

---

# Windsurf Instruction

Before generating any case study copy:

1. Read this file
2. Match the tone of surrounding content
3. Remove AI sounding phrasing
4. Remove all hyphens and em dashes
5. Keep sentences natural and direct
6. Prioritize clarity over sophistication
